Conversion & funnel optimisation

B2B conversion rate optimisation: more leads from the traffic you already have.

Lauren PearsonFounder, The Pearson Co.22 June 20267 min read

B2B conversion rate optimisation increases the percentage of business visitors who take a defined action: a demo request, form submission, or sales enquiry. Lauren Pearson's B2B CRO service identifies where qualified buyers drop off in your funnel and builds a prioritised improvement plan that increases pipeline without increasing ad spend.

Most B2B businesses put the bulk of their marketing budget into traffic: paid search, LinkedIn ads, content, SEO. The assumption is that more visitors produces more pipeline. It does, but only if the funnel converts them. B2B conversion rate optimisation works on the funnel itself, not on adding more traffic to one that leaks.

The B2B buying journey creates specific conversion challenges that consumer or ecommerce CRO does not address. Research by Forrester shows that B2B buyers complete 57% of their purchase journey before they speak to a sales representative. That means your website is doing most of the persuasion work before your team gets involved. Gartner research puts the typical B2B buying group at six to ten decision-makers, each arriving with different questions and different levels of awareness. A B2B CRO engagement has to account for that complexity, not just optimise a single call-to-action button.

This service sits within the broader conversion rate optimisation practice at The Pearson Co., focused specifically on companies selling to other businesses where the conversion goal is a qualified lead or booked meeting rather than a direct purchase.

Who this is for.

B2B conversion rate optimisation suits businesses with consistent website traffic that is not producing enough qualified conversations. The most common situations where this engagement adds clear value:

  • Your website generates sessions but enquiry volumes are low relative to the traffic. People are reading your content or browsing your service pages and then leaving without contacting you.
  • Your sales team is receiving leads that are not the right fit for what you offer. The funnel is attracting visitors but not qualifying them before they submit a form, so sales time is spent on conversations that go nowhere.
  • You are running paid search or LinkedIn campaigns and the cost per qualified lead is rising. You have tested ad variants but the problem is in the landing experience, not the ad itself.
  • Analytics shows a clear drop-off point, typically on a pricing page, contact form or specific service page, but the cause is not obvious and you do not want to make changes based on instinct alone.
  • The website was redesigned recently and conversion rates fell. The design may look better but the persuasion architecture was disrupted and the data shows it.
  • Multiple buyer personas visit the site and the current structure treats them all the same, producing a generic experience that does not speak well to any of them.

The engagement is not well-suited to businesses at an early stage where product-market fit is still being confirmed, or where traffic volumes are too low to produce reliable data. B2B CRO requires enough sessions on the key pages to make sound diagnostic decisions, typically a few hundred monthly visitors on the pages being worked on.

What is included.

A B2B CRO engagement covers the full path from first visit to a qualified sales conversation. The four areas of focus:

  • Funnel audit and analytics review. A structured analysis of where business visitors enter the site, which pages they visit, where they stop, and why conversion events happen at the rate they do. This includes reviewing goal tracking to confirm the analytics are measuring the right things. Misconfigured conversion goals are one of the most common reasons B2B companies believe their conversion rate is lower than it actually is.
  • Buyer persona and messaging audit. B2B purchases involve multiple people with different questions and different roles in the decision. The audit checks whether the site addresses each decision-maker's concerns, whether the value proposition is clear enough for a visitor who has not had a sales conversation yet, and whether the trust signals at each stage of the funnel match what that stage of the buyer journey requires.
  • Form and friction analysis. B2B contact forms are one of the biggest sources of conversion loss. Too many fields reduce submission rates; too few reduce lead quality. The analysis determines the right balance for your specific offer and audience, and identifies any technical or experience issues that make the form harder to complete than it needs to be.
  • Prioritised improvement backlog and testing programme. A ranked list of changes ordered by expected impact and implementation effort, covering both quick wins that can be done in days and structured A/B tests for changes where the right answer is not obvious from the data alone. The testing programme runs changes systematically, validates results against a significance threshold, and feeds learnings back into the next hypothesis.

How the engagement works.

The engagement starts with access to your analytics platform and a briefing on where you think the biggest conversion problems are. Two to three weeks of diagnostic work come before any changes are proposed.

Phase one: diagnostic (weeks one to three). Analytics review, session recording analysis where available, a structured walkthrough of the site as a first-time buyer in your target segment, and a review of the lead data your sales team currently receives. The output is a diagnostic report covering key drop-off points and their likely causes, a persona-path map showing how different buyer types navigate the site, and a ranked backlog of improvements.

Phase two: quick wins (weeks three to six). Implementing changes that do not require formal testing: clearer calls to action, improved trust signal placement, form simplification, page-speed fixes, and mobile experience improvements. These are tracked in before/after analytics rather than through A/B tests, as their impact is directional rather than marginal.

Phase three: structured testing (weeks six onwards). A/B testing higher-impact changes where the correct answer needs to be validated. Each test runs to statistical significance. Results are reviewed fortnightly: winning variants are rolled out, losing variants are documented, and the learning informs the next test.

Where conversion problems turn out to be upstream of the website, such as misaligned messaging across channels or weak lead routing between marketing and sales, the broader revenue operations service addresses those structural issues alongside the funnel work.

Outcomes and proof.

The measure of a B2B CRO engagement is a sustained improvement in the conversion rate of qualified leads from existing traffic. In practice, that means:

  • A higher percentage of website visitors submitting a contact form, requesting a demo, or taking another defined first step in the sales process, without a corresponding increase in ad spend.
  • Better lead quality: a higher proportion of enquiries fitting the ideal customer profile, which reduces the time sales spends on conversations that will not close.
  • A clear funnel map that shows where each buyer persona enters, what they need to see at each stage, and where the conversion points are. This means future site changes can be made against a model rather than based on gut feel.
  • Validated changes in place: improvements that have been tested and confirmed to work, not assumed to work because they follow a best-practice checklist.
  • An ongoing testing pipeline so conversion rate improvement continues after the initial engagement rather than stopping when the engagement ends.

Common questions.

What is B2B conversion rate optimisation?

B2B conversion rate optimisation (B2B CRO) is the process of increasing the percentage of business visitors to your website who take a desired action, typically a demo request, form submission or sales enquiry, rather than leaving without engaging. Unlike ecommerce CRO, the goal is not a direct purchase but a qualified entry point into the sales process. B2B CRO focuses on lead quality as much as lead volume, because driving unqualified enquiries wastes sales time.

What is a good B2B conversion rate?

B2B conversion rates vary by industry, offer type and traffic source. A common benchmark for B2B lead generation pages is 2-5%, with top-performing pages on very targeted paid traffic reaching 10-15%. The more useful benchmark is what your own rate is doing over time relative to the quality of leads it produces. A higher volume of poor-fit enquiries is not better than a lower volume of well-qualified ones.

How is B2B CRO different from ecommerce CRO?

B2B buying decisions typically involve 6-10 decision-makers, according to Gartner, with sales cycles measured in weeks or months rather than minutes. B2B CRO focuses on qualifying visitors early, building trust through case studies and social proof, reducing form friction without sacrificing lead quality, and structuring the site so that different buyer personas find the right path. It is less about micro-optimising a checkout and more about guiding a considered purchase decision toward a sales conversation.

How long does B2B CRO take to show results?

Initial diagnostic work takes two to three weeks. Quick wins, such as form simplification, clearer calls to action and improved trust signals, can show results within a month. A full testing programme, where changes are validated before being rolled out, typically takes three to six months to produce statistically reliable conversion rate improvements. The longer your sales cycle, the more lead time is needed to see the downstream impact on closed revenue.

Do I need B2B CRO if I already have a reasonable volume of leads?

Volume and quality are different problems. If your sales team is spending time on enquiries that do not fit your ideal customer profile, a B2B CRO engagement can improve the qualification signals in your funnel so that a higher percentage of leads are worth pursuing. If you are planning to increase paid acquisition spend, getting your existing funnel in better shape first means you get more from every pound you put into traffic.

What conversion points does the B2B CRO service cover?

The engagement covers the full path from website visit to a qualified sales conversation: landing pages, product or service pages, calls to action, contact and demo request forms, content download gates and pricing or packaging pages. It also covers the handoff between marketing and sales, including how lead data is captured, scored and routed, which affects conversion quality even when it does not appear directly on a website analytics report.
