CRM implementation & selection

A CRM audit that tells you exactly what's wrong, and what to fix first.

Lauren PearsonFounder, The Pearson Co.19 June 20267 min read

A CRM audit is a structured review of your CRM's data quality, pipeline configuration, workflow build and team usage. Lauren Pearson's CRM health check delivers a written report with a prioritised fix list: what's broken, what's being wasted and what to do first. Typical engagement: one to two days.

Most businesses that have had a CRM for more than a year live with a quiet doubt about what's actually in it. The pipeline report doesn't quite match what the sales team says is in play. Contacts are duplicated. Automations fire at unexpected moments. Leadership makes decisions from data they trust in public and quietly second-guess in private.

A CRM audit is a diagnostic that resolves that doubt. It tells you what's actually wrong, why it's wrong and what to do about it, in a sequence that makes sense for the business. It is not a consultancy project with a six-week timeline. It is a focused piece of work that produces a usable output within a week.

Who a CRM audit is for.

The businesses that benefit most have had a CRM for a year or more and can no longer fully trust what's in it. The signs are consistent: the pipeline report doesn't match what the team reports in the weekly call; duplicate records surface at inconvenient moments; automations were built by someone who has since left, and no one is entirely sure what they do; leadership is making forecasts from data they privately doubt.

A CRM audit is also the right starting point if you are about to invest in a CRM upgrade or platform switch and want to know what you are carrying into the next system. Bad data in an old CRM becomes bad data in a new one unless it is addressed before the migration.

Other situations where an audit is the right first step:

  • You have just hired or promoted a new sales leader who needs an honest view of the current state before committing to a RevOps programme.
  • Your sales team says the CRM doesn't fit how they work, but you're not sure whether that is a configuration problem or a training problem. The audit tells you which it is.
  • You have recently acquired a business and inherited their CRM alongside their team.
  • You are preparing for a funding round and want to be confident the pipeline data can stand up to investor questions.

What a CRM audit includes.

The audit covers four areas. Each produces a scored finding and a specific recommendation.

Data quality and completeness. How complete are your contact and deal records? Validity's 2023 State of CRM Data Health report found that 44% of businesses cite incomplete or inaccurate records as their biggest CRM challenge, and that the average contact record is missing at least 40% of the fields that matter for reporting. The audit looks at field completion rates, duplicate rates and whether data coming in from web forms, email integrations and other sources is arriving cleanly.

Pipeline configuration. Does your pipeline match how you actually sell? Misconfigured pipelines are one of the most common causes of reporting problems. Stage names that don't correspond to real decisions, exit criteria that no one enforces, deal weighting that bears no relationship to close probability: these are structural problems, not usage problems. The audit checks each of these and flags where the configuration is working against the sales motion rather than supporting it.

Workflow and automation health. Which automations are live, which are broken and which are doing something nobody intended? CRM workflows accumulate over time. A business that has been on HubSpot or Salesforce for two years often has dozens of active workflows, some of which contradict each other. The audit maps what is live, what fires correctly and what can be safely retired or consolidated.

Team usage and adoption. Logging rates, last-active dates and activity data tell you whether the team is using the system or working around it. Average CRM feature adoption in B2B businesses sits at around 26% of available functionality (Validity, 2023). If key deal fields are consistently empty and activity logging has gaps, the data problem is upstream of the CRM itself, and fixing the configuration will not solve it.

The deliverable is a written report with a scoring summary for each area and a prioritised fix list: the three things that will make the biggest difference, in the order that makes sense to do them.

How the engagement works.

A CRM audit runs in one to two days of diagnostic work, depending on system size. No lengthy kickoffs or extended project plans. The process is:

  1. Access. Read-only access to your CRM is sufficient. Admin rights are not required.
  2. Diagnostic. Working through the four audit areas directly in your system, pulling data and testing workflows as they stand.
  3. Interview. A 60-minute call with you or your sales lead to understand what is causing friction and what decisions the audit findings need to support.
  4. Report and debrief. A written report with findings and the prioritised fix list, followed by a 45-minute debrief call to walk through the recommendations and agree on next steps.

At the end, you have a clear picture of what to fix, in what order, and roughly what each fix involves. Whether you implement the recommendations yourself, with your internal team, or with Lauren as a follow-on implementation partner is your decision.

Outcomes and what a good audit delivers.

The typical outcome of a CRM audit is a clear answer to a question leadership has been carrying for a while: is the problem the data, the configuration or the people? Usually it is all three, in different proportions, and the value of the audit is that it sequences the fix correctly. Trying to fix adoption before fixing the pipeline configuration is the most common mistake, and it fails reliably.

Gartner's 2021 research on data quality costs found that poor data costs organisations an average of $12.9 million per year in wasted resource, missed revenue and bad decisions. The number is large partly because the damage is invisible: you don't see the deals that didn't close because the follow-up wasn't logged, or the forecast that was wrong because the weighting hadn't been updated.

The businesses that get the most from an audit are the ones that do something with the report. A written document that sits in a shared drive is not a useful investment. The audit is designed to produce three to five specific, implementable actions. Most clients complete the first action within a week of the debrief call.

If the findings point to a full CRM reconfiguration or a platform switch, the CRM implementation consultant engagement is the natural next step. If the audit confirms the system is broadly sound but adoption is low, the relevant conversation is about training and process, which the CRM implementation and selection page addresses.

Common questions.

How much does a CRM audit cost?

Pricing depends on the size and complexity of your CRM. For most founder-led businesses with one pipeline and a team of five to thirty people, an audit runs for one to two days at a day rate. Contact The Pearson Co. directly for a fixed-price quote based on your system size and what you need to understand.

What is a CRM health check?

A CRM health check and a CRM audit are the same thing: a structured review of your CRM's data quality, pipeline setup, workflow configuration and team usage. The deliverable is a written report with a prioritised fix list. "Health check" is often used for a shorter or more targeted review; a full audit covers all four areas.

How long does a CRM audit take?

Most audits run in one to two days of diagnostic work, followed by a written report and a debrief call. The timeframe from initial call to debrief is usually five to seven working days. For larger systems with multiple pipelines or complex integrations, the diagnostic takes longer and will be scoped accordingly.

Do I need to fix the issues myself after the audit?

No. The report gives you three options: fix it yourself using the detail in the report, brief your internal team to do it, or engage Lauren to implement the changes as a follow-on project. Most clients do a combination: handle the quick wins internally and scope the more complex changes as a separate engagement.

Which CRM platforms do you audit?

HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive and Zoho CRM are the most common. For other platforms, check on the initial call. The diagnostic approach is largely the same across platforms, though the specific reporting and admin tools differ.

What is the difference between a CRM audit and a RevOps review?

A CRM audit focuses on the CRM itself: data quality, pipeline setup, workflow configuration and adoption. A RevOps review is broader, covering how your revenue systems connect to each other and to your sales process. A CRM audit is often the right first step before a RevOps engagement, because it establishes the current baseline before any strategy work begins.
