Fractional sales leadership

A fractional CRO who aligns your revenue engine around one number.

Lauren PearsonFounder, The Pearson Co.17 June 20267 min read

A fractional CRO aligns marketing, sales and customer success under one revenue strategy, part-time. Lauren Pearson works as a fractional chief revenue officer for scaling businesses that need someone to own the whole revenue engine and the number that runs across all three functions.

When a business misses its revenue number, the cause is rarely just the sales team. Usually the problem is spread across three functions: marketing is generating the wrong kind of demand, sales is converting it inconsistently, and customer success is not catching churn before it shows up in the financials. Each team has a different read on what is wrong, and nobody sits above all three with the authority to fix it.

A fractional CRO (chief revenue officer) does exactly that. They take accountability for the whole revenue engine on a part-time basis: how marketing generates demand, how sales converts it, and how customer success protects and grows it. The remit is wider than a sales director's and sits at the level of the C-suite, but at a cost and commitment level that suits a scaling business rather than a large enterprise.

The fractional sales leadership page sets out how the CRO and sales director roles relate to each other and how both sit within the broader leadership model. If the gap is specifically inside the sales team rather than across revenue, the fractional sales director engagement is the more focused fit.

Who a fractional CRO is for.

The clearest fit is a B2B SaaS or professional services business that has separate marketing, sales and customer success functions but no shared revenue strategy connecting them. Marketing has targets. Sales has targets. Customer success has targets. None of them are derived from the same model, and when the revenue number is missed, each function points at the others.

The specific signals that a CRO is needed rather than a sales director: marketing and sales do not agree on what a qualified lead looks like. The handoff from sales to customer success is inconsistent, and early churn can be traced back to deals that should not have closed. The net revenue retention number is not tracked, or it is tracked but nobody owns it. The leadership team cannot give an investor a coherent answer about where next year's growth is coming from.

It also fits businesses preparing for a significant fundraise. At Series B and beyond, investors want to understand the revenue model across all three functions: what the acquisition economics look like, what is retained and what expands. A fractional CRO puts the structure and data in place for that conversation before it happens.

Occasionally, the fit is a business with a solid VP of sales already in place but no one thinking about the revenue picture above that level. The VP of sales owns the sales team. The head of marketing owns demand. But there is no one whose job it is to make them work together from a shared strategy. A fractional CRO fills that gap without displacing the existing leaders.

What a fractional CRO engagement includes.

A fractional CRO engagement covers the whole revenue engine across five areas.

Revenue strategy

The annual number broken down by source: new business, expansion from existing customers and the net revenue retention target. Most businesses set a new business target and largely ignore the expansion and retention mechanics until they become a problem. A fractional CRO sets all three from the start and makes sure each function knows what it owns.

Go-to-market alignment

A shared ideal customer profile across marketing, sales and customer success. Agreed definitions of a marketing-qualified lead, a sales-qualified opportunity and a customer health score. Handoff SLAs between functions so no deal or renewal falls between teams. Without this, each function is optimising for its own metric rather than the revenue outcome.

Sales process and pipeline

The same work a fractional sales director does inside the sales function: stage definitions, qualifying criteria, CRM configuration and forecast discipline. In a CRO engagement this work sits within the wider context of the marketing-to-sales handoff, so the pipeline reflects real demand rather than whatever marketing happens to be sending over.

Customer success and retention

A review of churn: when it happens, what the leading indicators were, and how the handoff from sales to customer success affects early retention. Expansion triggers: which signals in the customer base indicate readiness for an upsell, and who is accountable for acting on them. Most businesses track acquisition carefully and retention loosely. A fractional CRO changes that balance.

Revenue reporting and board preparation

A unified view of the funnel from first touch to retained customer: marketing pipeline contribution, sales conversion by stage, customer health scores and net revenue retention. Built across the CRM and reporting stack so the whole leadership team works from one set of numbers. For boards and investors, this becomes the revenue narrative: where the growth is coming from, what is on track, and what needs attention.

How the engagement works.

A fractional CRO engagement typically runs at two days a week, sometimes more during the first month.

Month one is a revenue audit. Not just the sales pipeline, but all three functions: what marketing is generating and how qualified it is, what the sales conversion looks like and where deals are being lost, and what the current retention economics are. The output is a clear picture of where the revenue engine is working and where it is leaking, with a prioritised plan for what to fix and in what order.

Months two to four are alignment. Getting marketing, sales and customer success working from the same ideal customer profile, the same lead and opportunity definitions, and the same revenue number. This involves working directly with the heads of each function, not just with the founder. It is operational work: agreeing the handoffs, fixing the CRM so it reflects the process, and putting in place the reporting that everyone owns.

From month four, the engagement moves to execution and embedding: running the revenue cadence (monthly revenue reviews, quarterly planning across all three functions), coaching the function leaders, and building out whatever is still missing. That might include hiring a VP of sales, a head of marketing or a head of customer success, or developing the people already in those roles.

The engagement ends when the revenue engine is self-sufficient or a full-time CRO is the right next hire. Most fractional CRO engagements run six to twelve months. Some businesses keep a fractional CRO on a light retainer for board reporting and quarterly planning after the main build is complete.

Outcomes and what the business looks like at the end.

After a fractional CRO engagement, marketing, sales and customer success work from the same ideal customer profile, with clear SLAs between functions and a shared understanding of what the revenue number means for each team.

The board reporting changes. Instead of a sales update and a separate marketing report that do not connect, the revenue review covers the whole funnel with consistent definitions and a clear picture of where the number is coming from. Investors can ask questions about pipeline coverage, net revenue retention and acquisition economics and get a coherent answer, not three different ones from three different people.

In practical terms, churn drops because the signals are spotted earlier and owned by someone. Expansion revenue grows because it is planned rather than accidental. The cost of acquiring new customers looks better in the context of what retained customers are worth over time. Those three things do not happen without someone whose job it is to own all three at once.

Common questions.

What is a fractional CRO?

A fractional CRO (chief revenue officer) is a senior revenue leader who works part-time, typically one to two days a week, and takes accountability for the whole revenue engine: how marketing generates demand, how sales converts it and how customer success protects and grows it. They own the revenue number across all three functions without the cost of a full-time C-suite hire.

What is the difference between a fractional CRO and a fractional chief revenue officer?

They are the same role. CRO stands for chief revenue officer. Both terms describe the same position: a senior leader accountable for the revenue strategy across marketing, sales and customer success. Fractional means they work part-time, on a retainer, rather than as a full-time employee.

How is a fractional CRO different from a fractional sales director?

A fractional sales director works inside the sales function: pipeline, process, team management and quota. A fractional CRO takes a wider remit across the whole revenue engine, including how marketing generates and hands over leads and how customer success protects retention. If the gap is specifically inside sales, a sales director is usually the right fit. If the gap is across revenue, a CRO is needed.

Do I need a fractional CRO or a fractional VP of sales?

If marketing and customer success are working reasonably well and the gap is inside the sales team, a fractional VP of sales or sales director is the more focused fit. If the marketing-to-sales handoff is broken, you have a churn problem you cannot diagnose, or you need someone to own the revenue number across all three functions, a fractional CRO is the right scope.

How long does a fractional CRO engagement last?

Most engagements run six to twelve months. Month one is a revenue audit. Months two to four are alignment across marketing, sales and CS. From month four, the engagement focuses on execution, coaching the function leaders and embedding the revenue cadence. Some businesses keep a fractional CRO on a light retainer for board reporting after the main build.

How much does a fractional CRO cost?

Priced on a monthly retainer based on days per week and the scope of the engagement. A fractional CRO costs significantly less than the salary, bonus, equity and on-costs of a full-time CRO. The starting point is a discovery call to understand the revenue engine, where the gaps are and what the engagement needs to achieve.

Can a fractional CRO help us prepare for a funding round?

Yes. At Series B and beyond, investors want to understand who owns the revenue strategy, how marketing, sales and customer success connect, and what the net revenue retention looks like. A fractional CRO puts the structure, data and narrative in place for that conversation: a clean revenue model, credible pipeline coverage and a retention story that holds up to scrutiny.
