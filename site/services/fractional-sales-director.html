Fractional sales leadership

A fractional sales director who builds the process your team can actually run.

Lauren PearsonFounder, The Pearson Co.17 June 20267 min read

A fractional sales director takes ownership of the sales function part-time: setting strategy, managing the pipeline, coaching the team and building the process. Lauren Pearson works as a fractional head of sales for founder-led businesses that need senior sales leadership without the cost of a full-time hire.

When a sales team underperforms, the instinct is often to hire. Another rep, a different rep, a better rep. But the most common reason a sales team misses target is not a hiring problem. It is a process and leadership problem: no clear qualifying criteria, no coaching cadence, no one to hold the forecast to account. A fractional sales director fixes that without the cost of a full-time executive hire.

A fractional sales director (also called a fractional head of sales) is a senior sales leader who works with your business on a part-time basis, typically one to two days a week. They take accountability for the sales function the way a full-time hire would: owning the pipeline, managing the team, setting the targets and making sure the process is something a rep can run without the founder in the room.

For a broader view of how the fractional model works and where it sits within senior sales and revenue leadership, the fractional sales leadership page covers the full picture, including when you need a sales director versus when the CRO remit makes more sense.

Who a fractional sales director is for.

The clearest fit is a founder-led business where the sales team exists but the leadership layer does not. You have one, two or three reps. Some are hitting target, some are not. The founder is still the best closer in the business, not because they want to be, but because when a deal goes quiet, they are the one who knows what to do about it.

That model does not scale. The founder's calendar is already the constraint on growth. Adding more reps without adding the leadership to manage them produces the same output from a larger team, at a higher cost.

Other situations where the model fits well. An early-stage team with one or two reps who have been in role for three months or more and are underperforming, with no clear read on why. A business raising capital that needs a credible sales leader as part of the story: someone who can talk to the pipeline, the metrics and the go-to-market with authority. A company entering the US market from Europe that needs someone who understands the US sales motion and buyer expectations.

The fractional model typically suits businesses at revenue run rates between $1 million and $15 million, where a full-time VP of sales costs $200,000 or more per year including equity and the return is uncertain. A fractional sales director gives you the senior sales judgement for a fraction of that spend, on a monthly retainer you can scale or exit as conditions change.

It is not the right model if there is no product-market fit. A sales director can build a process and coach a team, but cannot manufacture demand that is not there. And it is not enough if you genuinely need a full-time leader running a large team, every day. Between those two points is where the model works.

What a fractional sales director engagement includes.

A fractional sales director engagement covers five areas inside the sales function.

Sales strategy and targets

Defining the ideal customer profile for the business: who buys, who does not, and what the commercial signals are that distinguish one from the other. Agreeing the sales motion (inbound, outbound, partner-led or a blend), any territory or segment structure, and a number the team can be credibly held to. Targets set without a clear ideal customer profile and a defined motion are almost always wrong.

Pipeline and CRM process

Building or fixing the pipeline in the CRM: stage definitions, qualifying criteria, and the forecast discipline to move from gut instinct to evidence. The sales pipeline management framework sits at the centre of this. A fractional sales director ensures the CRM reflects the agreed process rather than the default vendor setup, and that the data is worth trusting.

Team management

Weekly pipeline reviews, rep coaching, performance conversations and, where needed, hiring decisions. Managing a sales team is a daily job at full scale; a fractional leader does it on a part-time schedule by running a tight cadence rather than being present in every call. The goal is a team that knows its targets, understands what qualified looks like, and has a coaching structure when it falls behind.

Sales reporting

Putting in place the reports that answer the questions a founder or investor actually asks: conversion rates by stage, pipeline coverage, average deal velocity and forecast accuracy. These come from the CRM once the process and data are right, and they replace the weekly verbal update that nobody is sure how to interpret.

Onboarding and sales enablement

When new reps join, they need a structure to ramp into. A fractional sales director builds that: a 30-60-90 day plan, the qualification framework, the call structure, and the product and competitive knowledge needed to carry a deal from first contact to close. An onboarded rep who knows the process from day one is a different hire from one who learns by watching the founder close deals.

How the engagement works.

Most fractional sales director engagements run at one to two days a week on a monthly retainer. The first phase is heavier; later it tapers as the team becomes self-sufficient.

The first two to four weeks are a diagnosis. Reviewing the pipeline, the team's activity, the CRM data, and the deals won and lost in the previous six to twelve months. Understanding what the founder knows instinctively about the best customers and the sales process, and making it explicit enough to hire into and coach against. This is the work that most sales leaders skip, and it is why most sales processes fail to stick.

Months two to four are the build phase. Setting the strategy and targets, configuring the CRM to the agreed process, establishing the weekly pipeline review cadence, coaching the existing team and, where there are gaps, defining the next hire and running the process to find them. This is the most intensive period in the engagement.

From month four or five, the focus shifts to embedding. The team runs the process. The forecast is consistent. The founder is no longer the first call when a deal stalls. The fractional leader either steps back to a quarterly oversight retainer or hands over to a full-time hire, with the role defined, the process documented and the metrics in place.

Total engagement length is typically three to nine months, depending on the size of the team and where the business starts. Some continue with a light retainer for quarterly planning after the main build is complete.

Outcomes and what changes by the end.

By the end of a fractional sales director engagement, three things should be true.

The sales motion is documented and does not depend on the founder. Any competent rep should be able to understand the ideal customer, run the process and know what a qualified opportunity looks like without asking for guidance. That is the first test.

The pipeline is trusted. Not just by the sales team, but by the founder and any investors looking at the business. Deals in the pipeline reflect real opportunities at the right stage, the forecast is defensible and the conversion metrics tell a consistent story over time.

The team has a performance cadence. Weekly pipeline reviews happen. Reps know their targets and have a qualifying framework. There is a coaching structure so a rep who is off-target gets the right help early rather than a performance conversation six months too late.

The longer-term benefit is that the eventual full-time hire is a much lower-risk decision. They step into a working system: a defined role, a documented process, a clean CRM and a team that already knows what good looks like. That is a very different position from bringing in a VP of sales to a blank page and asking them to figure it out.

Common questions.

What does a fractional sales director do?

A fractional sales director takes ownership of the sales function part-time: setting the sales strategy and targets, defining the process and pipeline stages, managing and coaching the sales team, and putting in place the reporting that keeps the founder and investors informed. The focus is inside the sales function, not across marketing or customer success.

How is a fractional sales director different from a fractional head of sales?

They are the same role. Fractional sales director and fractional head of sales are used interchangeably. Both describe a senior sales leader who works part-time, owns the sales function and is accountable for the team's performance. The title that fits depends on the company's own naming conventions.

How many days per week does a fractional sales director work?

Most engagements run at one to two days a week. The first phase, which covers diagnosis and process build, is usually heavier. Later, as the team runs the process and the cadence is established, the engagement can taper to one day or less. The right level depends on the size of the team and the starting point.

When should I hire a fractional sales director rather than wait for a full-time hire?

When the sales team is underperforming and the founder cannot pinpoint why, when early reps are missing target without a clear diagnosis, or when the pipeline depends on the founder closing the important deals. A fractional sales director fixes the process before you commit to a full-time hire, so the permanent leader steps into a working system.

Can a fractional sales director help us hire our first VP of sales?

Yes. One of the clearest benefits of a fractional engagement is that by the time you are ready for a full-time hire, the role is defined, the process is documented and the metrics are in place. A fractional sales director can write the job description, define what good looks like and sit on the interviews with the founder.

What does a fractional sales director engagement cost?

Priced on a monthly retainer based on days per week and the scope of the engagement. The starting conversation is a discovery call to understand the team, the pipeline and what needs to change, at which point a structure and fee are proposed. The cost is a fraction of the all-in cost of a full-time sales director.

Do you work with US-based businesses?

Yes. The fractional sales director engagement suits US founder-led businesses, particularly SaaS and professional services teams scaling from early revenue to a repeatable go-to-market. All work runs remotely. Where in-person time is useful during the build phase, it can be arranged.
