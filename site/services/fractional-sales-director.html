Fractional sales leadership

A fractional sales director who builds your sales function from the ground up.

Lauren PearsonFounder, The Pearson Co.18 June 20267 min read

A fractional sales director is a senior sales leader who works with your business part-time, typically one to three days a week. They set the sales strategy, build and document the process, manage the pipeline and coach the team, at a fraction of the cost of a full-time VP of Sales hire. Lauren Pearson works in this role for founder-led and scaling B2B businesses.

When a founder closes every deal, the business is not scalable. The sales process lives in the founder's head, new reps do not know what good looks like, and growth depends on one person's capacity rather than a system the team can run. When the founder steps back from a deal, it either stalls or closes only because the founder stepped back in.

A fractional sales director is how you change that without a full-time executive hire. A senior sales leader works with your business one to three days a week: setting the strategy, building the process, managing the pipeline and coaching the team. It is the capability of a VP of Sales on a monthly retainer rather than a permanent payroll. The wider fractional sales leadership context is covered on the fractional sales leadership page, including how this compares to a fractional head of sales and when a fractional CRO is the better fit.

Who this is for.

The businesses that benefit most from a fractional sales director share a pattern. They have product-market fit and some revenue, but the sales function has not yet been built as a function. The founder is still the closest thing to a sales leader. Leads are coming in but conversion is unpredictable. There may be a small sales team, but without a clear process or management structure, results depend on individual effort rather than a shared method.

There are a few specific situations where a fractional engagement makes the most sense.

The first is a business where founder-led selling has hit a ceiling. Revenue is growing but everything runs through the founder. Taking the founder out of the close means deals slow down or stop. The fractional sales director's job is to extract the process from the founder's head, document it, and build the team's ability to run it independently.

The second is a business that has hired its first or second sales rep but those reps are not performing without founder involvement. A fractional sales director works out whether the issue is a process problem, a coaching problem, a territory problem or a hiring mistake, and acts on it directly rather than speculating.

The third is a business preparing for a fundraise. Investors at Series A want to see a repeatable sales motion, a credible pipeline and someone with seniority who can answer detailed questions about the commercial model. A fractional sales director can put that in place without a permanent hire on the balance sheet.

For US-market businesses, the cost difference is significant. A full-time VP of Sales carries a base salary above $175,000 before bonus and equity (Glassdoor, 2024). A fractional engagement for one to two days per week typically costs $6,000 to $12,000 per month. The access to seniority is comparable; the commitment is not.

What is included.

A fractional sales director engagement covers five areas.

Sales process design. Before anything is coached or managed, the sales process needs to exist as something written down. That means documenting the stages, exit criteria, qualification framework and what good looks like at each point in the deal. Most businesses do not have this in a form that a new rep can follow. When the process is documented, the team can be trained on it, the CRM can be configured to match it, and gaps in rep performance can be diagnosed against it rather than guessed at.

Pipeline management. Weekly pipeline reviews, deal stage discipline and the practice of keeping the forecast grounded in what is actually in the business. The fractional sales director runs the pipeline review, sets the standard for what gets moved forward and what does not, and ensures the data in the CRM reflects reality. If you also need the CRM itself configured to support a new sales process, the CRM implementation and selection page covers how that works.

Team coaching. Rep performance reviews, call reviews, deal coaching and the feedback structures that improve close rates over time. This is not a training course; it is an ongoing coaching relationship built around the team's live deals and real challenges. The objective is a team that improves consistently rather than one that attends a course and then reverts to old habits.

Reporting and forecasting. A reliable forecast depends on clean pipeline data and a clear view of close rates, deal velocity and conversion by stage. The fractional sales director builds or fixes the reporting so that the number the business forecasts from is one it can believe. Reporting that cannot be trusted is reporting no one looks at.

Hiring preparation. If the business is approaching the point where a full-time sales director or VP makes sense, the fractional sales director helps define the role, builds the hiring brief and can assess candidates. Part of the value of a fractional arrangement is that when the full-time hire is made, the process already exists for them to run. They are not starting from nothing.

How the engagement works.

The engagement begins with a diagnosis period of two to four weeks.

During this period, Lauren reviews the current pipeline, close rates, deal cycle length, rep activity and CRM configuration. The objective is to understand what is already working, what is not, and where the biggest commercial gaps are before any changes are made. Changing things before the diagnosis is complete is how well-intentioned interventions cause disruption without producing results.

After the diagnosis, the engagement moves into the build phase. The first 90 days focus on the sales process document, the CRM configuration that reflects it, the pipeline review cadence, and the first round of rep coaching. These are the foundations. Without them, everything that follows sits on unstable ground.

The ongoing engagement then runs on a monthly retainer. One to three days per week, agreed upfront. The specific focus in any given month flexes with what the business needs: a heavy coaching month when a new rep joins, a forecasting focus before a board meeting, or a hiring brief month when the first full-time sales leader is being recruited.

The engagement can end when the founder is ready to make a permanent hire, when the business wants to run independently on the process that has been built, or when the arrangement evolves into a different scope. A clear exit is part of the design from the start, not something worked out at the end.

Outcomes and what you can expect.

By the end of the first 90 days, a business should have a documented sales process, a CRM that reflects how the team sells, a pipeline the founder can trust for forecasting, and reps who understand what good performance looks like. These are the conditions that make revenue growth predictable rather than dependent on luck or founder effort.

Over a six to twelve month engagement, the tangible changes are: a higher close rate from the same lead volume (because deals that are not going to close are qualified out earlier), a shorter deal cycle, and a sales team that can run its own pipeline review without the founder in the room.

The fractional model is also honest about its purpose. Part of the job is to build a process and a team capable of operating without the fractional leader. When that point is reached, it is a success. The business either continues without external sales leadership or hires a permanent person into a role that already has a process, a team and a track record. That is a substantially better position to recruit from than an empty role with no process in place.

Common questions.

What is a fractional sales director?

A fractional sales director is a senior sales leader who works with your business part-time, typically one to three days a week on a monthly retainer. They set the sales strategy, design and document the sales process, manage the pipeline and coach the team, at a fraction of the cost of a full-time VP of Sales hire.

How does a fractional sales director differ from a full-time VP of sales?

The scope of work is comparable; the commitment and cost are not. A full-time VP of Sales in the US carries a base salary above $175,000 before bonus and equity. A fractional sales director works one to three days per week on a monthly retainer, suited to businesses that need senior-level commercial leadership but are not yet ready for a permanent executive hire.

How much does a fractional sales director cost?

A fractional engagement is billed on a monthly retainer tied to the number of days per week agreed at the start. It is substantially lower than the all-in cost of a full-time hire once salary, bonus, equity and on-costs are included, and it can be scaled up or down as the business changes.

What results can I expect in the first 90 days?

The first 90 days typically deliver a documented sales process, a CRM configured to reflect how the team actually sells, a pipeline the founder can forecast from, and reps with clear expectations for what good performance looks like. These are the foundations. Without them, everything else is guesswork.

How do I know when to hire a full-time sales director instead?

When the sales team is large enough that it needs daily management rather than weekly oversight, or when the business has the pipeline volume and predictability to justify the full-time cost, a permanent hire makes sense. Part of the fractional engagement is building toward that point so the full-time hire inherits a working process, not a blank slate.

Can you work with teams in the US market?

Yes. The engagement works remotely and the process design, coaching and pipeline management do not require physical presence. Lauren works with businesses based in the UK, UAE, Europe and the US, with a particular focus on founder-led B2B teams at the pre-Series A and Series A stage.
