Fractional sales leadership

A fractional sales director who builds the function while closing the gap.

Lauren PearsonFounder, The Pearson Co.19 June 20267 min read

A fractional sales director gives a founder-led business senior sales leadership part-time. Lauren Pearson takes on the VP Sales role: setting strategy, building the team structure, running the pipeline process and coaching the team, for a fraction of a full-time hire. Typical commitment: two to three days a week.

There is a gap that most founder-led businesses fall into somewhere between five and twenty sales-related hires. Revenue is growing, but it depends on the founder being personally involved in deals. The team exists but has no structured pipeline process or coaching cadence. A full-time VP Sales would fix this, but the business is not yet at the revenue or scale that justifies the hire.

A fractional sales director fills that gap. Two or three days a week, a senior sales leader works alongside the team: setting the strategy, managing the pipeline process, running the coaching conversations and building the structure that lets the business grow without the founder at the centre of every deal.

For the broader model and how it compares to other options, the fractional sales leadership page covers the full picture. This page is specific to the fractional sales director engagement.

Who a fractional sales director is for.

The businesses that benefit most have outgrown founder-led selling but have not yet built the commercial infrastructure to grow independently of the founder. The signs are specific: the founder is still involved in every large deal, the sales team runs on informal processes and personal relationships rather than a documented pipeline, and there is no structured coaching or forecasting cadence.

This model suits you if:

  • You are the founder and you are still closing most of the business. Revenue is growing but it depends on your personal involvement, and you know that has to change.
  • You have a sales team but no one managing them with a proper framework: quota structure, pipeline process, coaching cadence, forecasting.
  • You need someone who can hire and brief the first full-time Head of Sales when the business is ready, and who has a clear view on what that person should look like.
  • You are opening a new market or vertical and need senior commercial leadership to own the entry without a permanent hire for a function that may need reshaping once the market is understood.

This is not the right model if you primarily need someone to carry a large individual quota. A fractional sales director is a leadership and management role. Some deal involvement is normal, particularly at senior or enterprise level where a senior voice makes a difference. Being the primary closer is not.

What a fractional sales director includes.

An engagement with Lauren Pearson as a fractional sales director covers the key responsibilities of the VP Sales role.

Sales process and pipeline design. Defining or refining the sales stages, exit criteria, qualification framework and handover points from marketing to sales to customer success. The process needs to work for the business as it is today and have room to scale. A process that requires the founder to be involved at every stage is a founder dependency, not a sales process.

Pipeline management and forecasting. Running the weekly or bi-weekly pipeline review with the team. Setting the rhythm for deal reviews, identifying blockers early and building a forecast the business can plan from. Gartner research consistently shows that sales teams with a structured pipeline review cadence close at higher rates than those without one, even when the underlying lead quality is the same.

Team leadership and coaching. One-to-ones, call reviews, performance conversations, raising standards without losing good people. This is often where the fractional model adds the most value: a founder who is also carrying the Head of Sales role rarely has time to do this well. The team needs a dedicated leader who can invest in the coaching conversations without that time coming directly from deal activity.

Recruitment and hiring. If the brief includes building out the sales team, that means writing role specifications, screening candidates, running a structured interview process and making the hire recommendation. When the business is ready for a full-time VP Sales, the fractional director is the person who defines what that role should look like and how to find the right person for it.

RevOps and CRM alignment. Making sure the CRM, reporting and sales tools support the sales motion rather than just recording activity. If a CRM diagnostic is needed before strategy work makes sense, a CRM audit is the right starting point. How the engagement works.

Most fractional sales director engagements run on a two or three day per week basis, with a minimum commitment of three months to give the work enough runway to produce results. The structure is consistent across engagements:

The first month is diagnostic: understanding the team, the pipeline, the process gaps and where the quick wins are. This is not a lengthy audit. It is the practical work of observing deal reviews, sitting in on sales calls, reviewing the CRM data and having direct conversations with the team about what is and is not working.

Months two and three are execution: implementing the process changes, establishing the team rhythm and starting to see the reporting stabilise. By the end of month three, the pipeline review should be running without the founder's presence, and the forecast should be a number the business can plan from rather than a rough estimate.

The engagement is remote by default, with monthly in-person if the business wants it. The rhythm is agreed at the start and adjusted as the business's needs change.

Outcomes and what a good engagement delivers.

The outcomes worth measuring are not just revenue, though revenue is the eventual test. The more immediate measures are whether the sales function now operates independently of the founder, whether the team has a process it follows consistently, whether the forecast is trusted and whether the business is ready to make a full-time hiring decision.

That last point matters. Hiring a full-time Head of Sales before the process and team structure are in place often means the new hire inherits an undocumented, founder-dependent function and leaves within 18 months. Pavilion's 2024 State of Revenue Leadership report puts the median VP Sales tenure at B2B scale-ups at 18 to 24 months, with early churn the most common cause of wasted hiring cost. A fractional engagement builds the foundation before the permanent hire arrives, so they walk into something that can work rather than something that needs rebuilding.

If the challenge also spans marketing, customer success and revenue systems as a whole, the fractional CRO engagement covers that wider scope. The distinction between the two roles is straightforward: a fractional sales director owns the sales function; a fractional CRO owns the full revenue motion.

Common questions.

What is a fractional sales director?

A fractional sales director is a senior sales leader who works with a business on a part-time basis, typically two to three days a week. They take on the strategic and management responsibilities of a VP Sales or Head of Sales: setting strategy, managing the team, running pipeline reviews and building the sales process, without the cost of a permanent hire.

What is the difference between a fractional sales director and a fractional head of sales?

The terms are largely interchangeable. "Fractional head of sales" is more common in UK and European markets; "fractional sales director" is used more in the US. Both describe the same model: senior sales leadership, part-time, for a business that needs the capability without committing to a full-time hire.

How much does a fractional sales director cost?

Pricing varies by days per week, engagement length and scope. A fractional engagement typically runs at 20 to 40% of a full-time VP Sales equivalent salary for the same number of days, with no employer costs, no recruitment fee and no notice period. How long does a fractional sales director engagement last?

The minimum useful commitment is three months, to allow time for the diagnostic phase, process implementation and initial results. Many engagements run for six to twelve months. The endpoint is typically when the business is ready to bring in a full-time Head of Sales, or when a stable process is in place that needs less oversight.

Can a fractional sales director also close deals?

Some deal involvement is normal, particularly at the strategic or enterprise level where a senior voice makes a difference. But a fractional sales director is a leadership and management role, not a quota-carrying sales role. If you primarily need someone to generate and close business, a field sales or business development hire is the right answer.

When is the right time to hire a fractional sales director?

When the founder is still personally closing most of the business, the team has no formal pipeline or coaching structure, and revenue is at a level where a part-time senior leader is justified but a full-time hire is not. For most B2B businesses, that happens somewhere between £500k and £3m in annual revenue, though the right trigger is the team structure and founder dependency, not just the revenue figure.
