Conversion & funnel optimisation

Landing page optimisation: pages that convert what you send them.

Lauren PearsonFounder, The Pearson Co.22 June 20267 min read

Landing page optimisation is the process of improving the elements on a specific page, typically headline, copy, form and call to action, to increase the percentage of visitors who take the desired action. Lauren Pearson's landing page optimisation service audits your current pages, identifies the conversion blockers, and builds a testing plan that improves results from the traffic you already have.

A landing page is where paid traffic either pays off or it does not. You have spent budget to get someone there; landing page optimisation is the work that determines what happens next. The average landing page conversion rate across industries sits at around 2.35%, according to WordStream data. The top 25% of pages convert at 5.31% or above. That gap is not explained by the quality of the traffic. It is explained by the quality of the page.

Landing page optimisation focuses on a specific page or a small group of pages rather than a full website or funnel. That focus is what makes it distinct. The question is not "why does our site underperform overall?" but "why do visitors arriving on this specific page, from this specific source, not take this specific action?" That question has a more precise answer, and it leads to changes that can be tested quickly against a clear baseline.

This work sits within the broader conversion rate optimisation practice. It is the right starting point when you have a specific page, or a group of similar pages, where the gap between traffic and results is clear and you want to close it.

Who this is for.

Landing page optimisation is well-suited to any business running paid traffic, whether that is Google Ads, LinkedIn, Meta, or any other channel where the cost per click matters. The situations where this engagement adds immediate value:

  • You are running paid search campaigns and the cost per lead or cost per acquisition has risen. The ad click-through rates are reasonable but the landing page conversion rate is below where it should be for the spend to be profitable.
  • You have a campaign landing page that was built quickly, or repurposed from a general site page, and it does not match the specific message of the ads driving traffic to it. Message match between ad and landing page is one of the most consistent conversion levers.
  • You have multiple campaigns pointing to the same landing page, serving different audiences, and the page cannot serve all of them well. Splitting by audience almost always improves performance.
  • Analytics shows a high bounce rate on a key page, or a form completion rate well below typical benchmarks for your category, but you are not sure whether the problem is the headline, the copy, the form or the layout.
  • You are preparing to scale ad spend and want to ensure the landing experience is in good shape before pushing more budget through it. Fixing a leaking landing page before scaling is significantly more cost-effective than fixing it after.
  • A previous redesign of the landing page did not improve performance, or made it worse, and you need a data-led approach rather than another round of design changes based on preference.

What is included.

A landing page optimisation engagement covers every element that drives or blocks conversion on the page.

  • Page audit and conversion review. A structured analysis of the current page: headline clarity, offer presentation, form structure and length, call-to-action copy and placement, trust signals, social proof, page speed and mobile experience. The audit identifies the highest-priority conversion problems before any changes are made.
  • Message match analysis. For paid traffic, the relationship between the ad copy and the landing page headline is one of the strongest predictors of conversion rate. This analysis checks whether there is a clear, specific match between what the ad promises and what the page delivers in the first five seconds. Where the match is weak, rewriting the headline or restructuring the page entry is usually the highest-impact change available.
  • Copy review and rewrite. Landing page copy fails conversion in specific, identifiable ways: too focused on features rather than outcomes, too long above the fold, using language the visitor does not use themselves, or missing the objection that is most likely to stop someone from acting. The copy review identifies these failures; the rewrite produces tested alternatives structured for the conversion action.
  • A/B test design and execution. For changes where the right answer is not obvious, structured A/B tests compare the current version against a defined alternative. Tests are designed to measure one variable at a time and run to statistical significance before a winner is called. The testing programme is prioritised by expected impact so the highest-value changes are tested first.
  • Technical and speed review. Page load speed affects conversion rates directly, particularly on mobile. The technical review identifies load-time issues, mobile rendering problems and any friction in the form submission process that is reducing completions.

How the engagement works.

The engagement starts with access to the page analytics and any existing test data. The audit phase takes one to two weeks before any changes are proposed.

Phase one: audit (weeks one to two). A full review of the current page performance, message match, copy, layout and technical factors. The output is an audit report covering the key conversion problems ranked by likely impact, and a testing backlog with specific hypotheses for each change.

Phase two: quick wins (weeks two to four). Implementing changes that do not require formal A/B testing: headline rewrites where the current headline is clearly failing, form simplification, trust signal repositioning, mobile fixes, and speed improvements. These are tracked in before/after analytics, with a defined observation period to confirm the direction of change.

Phase three: structured testing (weeks four onwards). A/B testing the higher-impact changes where the correct answer needs to be validated. Each test runs to statistical significance. Winning variants are implemented; the insights feed the next test. Reporting is fortnightly, with a clear view of what is in test, what the current results are, and what is next in the backlog.

Outcomes and proof.

The measure of a landing page optimisation engagement is a sustained improvement in the conversion rate of the specific page, tracked against the pre-engagement baseline. In practice, that looks like:

  • A higher percentage of visitors taking the desired action, whether that is submitting a form, requesting a call, signing up for a trial or clicking through to the next step in the funnel.
  • A lower cost per lead or cost per acquisition from paid campaigns pointing to the page, because the same ad spend produces more qualified responses.
  • Clear message match between ad copy and landing page: visitors arriving from a specific ad land on a page that immediately confirms they are in the right place and tells them what to do next.
  • A mobile experience that converts at a rate comparable to desktop. The mobile-desktop conversion gap is one of the most common and most fixable landing page problems.
  • A testing log that documents what was tested, what the results were, and why. This becomes a reference for future campaigns and a brief for future page builds, so the improvement compounds rather than starting from scratch each time.

Landing page optimisation produces the most sustained improvements when it is treated as an ongoing programme rather than a one-time fix. Pages that are regularly tested and refined against new traffic data consistently outperform those that are set once and left unchanged, particularly when the traffic source, audience or offer changes over time.

Common questions.

What is landing page optimisation?

Landing page optimisation is the process of improving the elements on a specific page, typically the headline, body copy, call to action, form, layout and social proof, to increase the percentage of visitors who take the desired action. It differs from broader CRO in that it focuses on a single page or a small group of pages rather than an entire site or funnel, and is particularly relevant for paid traffic where each click costs money and the landing experience determines whether that spend is profitable.

What is a good landing page conversion rate?

WordStream data puts the average landing page conversion rate across industries at around 2.35%, with the top 25% of pages converting at 5.31% or above. Top-performing pages in specific categories, particularly on highly targeted paid traffic, convert at 10% or more. The right benchmark depends on your offer, your traffic source and what the conversion action is. The more useful question is whether your page is performing above or below similar pages in your category.

What elements of a landing page have the biggest impact on conversion?

The headline is almost always the highest-impact element, because it determines whether a visitor stays or leaves in the first few seconds. After that, message match between the ad and the page, the clarity of the offer, the form length and the placement of trust signals (testimonials, logos, guarantees) consistently move conversion rates. Page load speed matters significantly too, particularly on mobile. The right order to test these depends on diagnostic data from the specific page.

How long does landing page optimisation take?

An initial audit takes one to two weeks. Quick wins, such as headline rewrites, form simplification and trust signal placement, can be implemented and producing results within a month. Formal A/B testing takes four to eight weeks per test to reach statistical significance, depending on traffic volume. The timeline for a full testing cycle covering the highest-priority changes is typically three to four months.

Do I need a separate landing page for each ad campaign?

For paid campaigns, dedicated landing pages almost always outperform sending traffic to a general website page. A dedicated landing page removes navigation distractions, matches the specific message of the ad, and focuses the visitor on a single action. The research consistently shows that message match between ad copy and landing page headline is one of the strongest predictors of conversion rate. One page per campaign is a reasonable default; one page per audience segment is better when traffic volumes support testing.

Should I redesign my landing page or just optimise what I have?

Usually, optimise first. A full redesign is expensive, takes time, and often produces a page that looks better but converts at the same rate or worse because the changes were not based on data. An optimisation-first approach identifies what is actually causing the conversion problem before committing to a rebuild. That said, if the diagnostic shows that the current page structure cannot be fixed incrementally, a redesign against a tested brief is a better investment than continued iteration.
