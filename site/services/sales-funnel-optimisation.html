Conversion & funnel optimisation

Sales funnel optimisation: fix the stages that lose deals.

Lauren PearsonFounder, The Pearson Co.23 June 20267 min read

Sales funnel optimisation is the process of auditing and improving every stage of the path from first click to closed deal. In B2B, that means the website and the post-form process: lead routing, CRM pipeline stages, follow-up speed and cadence, and qualification criteria. Fix those, and you convert more of the traffic you already have.

Where the funnel actually breaks.

Most businesses treat sales funnel optimisation as a website problem. They test button colours, rewrite headlines, and add trust badges. Sometimes that helps. But in B2B, the bigger losses happen after someone fills in a form.

A lead submits an enquiry at 11pm on a Tuesday. It arrives in a shared inbox. Someone picks it up Thursday morning, two days later. By that point, the prospect has spoken to two competitors and mentally moved on. The website did its job. The funnel did not.

Harvard Business Review research by Oldroyd et al. found that companies which respond to a web inquiry within one hour are nearly seven times more likely to qualify the lead than those that respond later. Seven times. That gap is not a marketing problem. It is a systems problem, and it sits entirely post-form.

This service addresses the full end-to-end funnel: from the ad or search result that starts the journey, through the website and landing page, across the form submit and CRM intake, and into the follow-up sequence that either converts the lead or loses them. It connects to the broader conversion rate optimisation work, but goes further into what happens inside your pipeline.

Who this is for.

This engagement suits B2B founders and revenue leaders who are generating leads but not closing enough of them, and who suspect the problem is somewhere between "form submitted" and "deal won".

Specifically, it is a good fit if any of these are true:

  • Leads come in but response times are inconsistent and untracked.
  • Your CRM has pipeline stages that were set up at launch and never revisited.
  • Sales reps decide individually how many times to follow up and when to give up.
  • You cannot tell, from data, where in the pipeline deals stall most often.
  • Marketing and sales argue about lead quality rather than measuring conversion rates at each stage.
  • Your close rate feels low relative to the volume of enquiries coming in.

It is also relevant if you are growing fast and need the funnel to hold at higher volume without adding headcount proportionally. A lead management setup that worked at 20 leads a month tends to break at 200.

The engagement works across SaaS, professional services, hospitality tech, and consulting businesses. The mechanics differ, but the pattern is consistent: post-form drop-off is where the money goes missing.

What is included.

The work starts with a full funnel audit. That means mapping every stage, measuring conversion rates between stages, and identifying where the attrition is highest. Not assumed. Measured.

Funnel stage audit and conversion mapping

We pull data from your CRM, marketing automation platform, and website analytics to build a stage-by-stage conversion rate map. Most clients see this clearly for the first time during this process. "We thought we had a traffic problem. Turns out we were converting 40% of enquiries to first calls but only 12% of first calls to proposals."

Lead routing review

How a lead gets from the form to the right person, in the right time frame, with the right context. We document the current state, identify gaps in routing logic, and redesign the workflow so every lead gets a consistent, timely first response. This typically involves CRM workflow rules, assignment logic, and notification setup. It connects to revenue operations at the systems level.

CRM pipeline stage redesign

Pipeline stages should reflect real buyer behaviour, not internal admin preferences. We review your current stages against the actual pattern of how your deals move, remove stages that create confusion, and add exit criteria so "Proposal Sent" means the same thing to every rep. This is the foundation of accurate forecasting.

Follow-up cadence and sequence design

What happens after the first response? Most B2B funnels have an answer for day one. Few have a consistent answer for day three, day seven, or day fourteen. We design a documented follow-up cadence: timing, channel mix, message intent at each touch, and a clear definition of when a lead is marked dead and why. This cadence is built into the CRM, not left to individual judgement.

Qualification criteria and lead scoring

Not every lead should get the same level of effort. We work with you to define what a qualified lead looks like for your business, build those criteria into the intake process, and set up scoring or tagging that lets reps prioritise their time. This reduces the friction between marketing and sales and shifts the conversation from "the leads are bad" to "here is what good looks like, and here is how we get more of it".

Website-to-funnel connection points

The website audit covers form placement, form length, thank-you page logic, and the messaging alignment between ad copy, landing page, and the first follow-up email. This is where the CRO strategy and the sales funnel work meet. A form that converts well but passes poor-quality lead data into the CRM creates downstream problems that no amount of follow-up speed can fix.

How a sales funnel optimisation engagement works.

The work runs in three phases over four to six weeks, depending on the complexity of your existing setup and how many data sources need integrating.

Phase 1: audit and diagnosis (weeks 1 and 2)

Access to your CRM, marketing automation, and web analytics. We build the conversion rate map, document the current lead routing and follow-up process, and interview one or two people who handle incoming leads day-to-day. The output is a prioritised problem list: where the losses are biggest, and where fixing them is most straightforward.

The customer journey mapping work informs this phase if it has already been completed. If not, we do a lighter version focused specifically on the buyer's path through the funnel.

Phase 2: design and build (weeks 2 to 4)

We redesign the pipeline stages, write the follow-up cadence, and build the lead routing workflows. Depending on your CRM, this means building automations directly (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive) or writing the specifications for your team to implement. The qualification criteria and lead scoring model are agreed in this phase and documented in a format your team can maintain.

Phase 3: handover and measurement setup (weeks 4 to 6)

We configure the reporting so you can track stage-by-stage conversion rates going forward. The handover documentation covers what was built, why, and how to adjust it as your business changes. We also set a 30-day check-in to review the early data and make adjustments before the engagement closes.

Outcomes and proof.

The most consistent result of sales funnel optimisation work is a reduction in lead response time. The Harvard Business Review research is a useful benchmark: the gap between responding in one hour and responding later is not marginal. Clients who implement proper lead routing and CRM notifications typically reduce average response time from days to under two hours, often under 30 minutes.

Beyond response time, the other common outcomes are:

  • Higher first-call booking rates. When the first follow-up message is timely, relevant, and reflects what the prospect actually asked about, more of them agree to a call.
  • Better forecast accuracy. When pipeline stages have clear exit criteria and reps use them consistently, the pipeline number becomes trustworthy. Founders stop discounting it by 50% before presenting to investors.
  • Shorter sales cycles. Defined follow-up cadences stop deals from going quiet mid-process. The number of deals that "just went cold" without a clear reason tends to drop.
  • Clearer attribution. When the funnel is instrumented properly, you can see which traffic sources produce leads that actually close, not just leads that fill in forms. That changes where you spend the marketing budget.

For businesses also working on top-of-funnel, the B2B conversion rate optimisation service addresses the earlier buyer journey stages, and SaaS conversion rate optimisation covers the specific patterns in product-led and trial-conversion funnels.

The goal across all of this is the same: stop losing deals that should have been won. The traffic is there. The interest is there. The funnel has to do its job.

Common questions.

What is the difference between sales funnel optimisation and conversion rate optimisation?

Conversion rate optimisation typically focuses on the website: landing pages, forms, button placement, and on-page messaging. Sales funnel optimisation covers the full journey from first click to closed deal, including what happens after the form is submitted: lead routing, CRM pipeline stages, follow-up cadence, and qualification. In B2B, the bigger losses are usually post-form, not pre-form.

How long does a sales funnel optimisation engagement take?

Most engagements run four to six weeks. The first two weeks are audit and diagnosis. Weeks two to four cover the redesign and build phase. The final phase is handover, reporting setup, and a 30-day check-in. More complex setups with multiple CRM integrations or large sales teams may take slightly longer.

Which CRM platforms do you work with?

HubSpot and Salesforce are the most common. Pipedrive, Zoho CRM, and Monday CRM are also covered. The audit and diagnostic work applies regardless of platform. For the build phase, automations and workflows are built directly in HubSpot and Salesforce. For other platforms, the deliverable is a documented specification your team can implement.

Do you need access to our sales data to do this work?

Yes. The audit phase requires read access to your CRM (to pull pipeline and stage conversion data), your web analytics (to measure pre-form drop-off), and your marketing automation platform if you use one. All data is handled confidentially. We work with whatever access level your IT or ops team can provide.

What if our CRM data is messy and inconsistent?

It usually is. Part of the audit is understanding where the data is reliable and where it is not. We work with what exists and flag the data quality issues that are distorting your picture. Cleaning up those issues often becomes part of the project scope, because you cannot measure funnel performance against data you cannot trust.

Can this be combined with revenue operations or lead management work?

Yes. Sales funnel optimisation naturally connects to revenue operations at the systems level (lead routing, CRM architecture, reporting) and to lead management for the intake and qualification process. Many clients start with the funnel audit and then roll into a broader RevOps engagement once the priority gaps are clear. The scope is agreed at the start and can flex as the work progresses.
